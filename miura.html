Lamborghini Miura SV — The Genesis of the Modern Supercar.

A Transverse Masterpiece

In 1966, the Miura didn't just change the game; it invented it. Before the Miura, performance cars were front-engined grand tourers. Lamborghini's engineers, working in their spare time, envisioned a car with a transverse-mounted V12 engine positioned directly behind the driver.

The result was Marcello Gandini's breathtaking design, featuring "eyelashes" on the headlights and a profile that remains one of the most beautiful in automotive history. The SV (Spinto Veloce) was the ultimate evolution, featuring flared rear arches and improved performance.
